Other Interests

Societal Impact of AI

Since August of 2022, I have been working with the Digital Humanities program on the societal impact of AI. My involvement with the program is around how environmental factors affect our trust and use of AI.

Digital Humanities (DH) tries to understand the relationship between human factors and emerging technologies. The good people at DH do a wonderful job pondering what comes next in a world where AI is ubiquitous. Previously, I have given talks on the role bias plays in determining AI safety and how AI affects our perception of privacy. I am deeply committed to understanding the social and ethical impact that AI has and ways to participate in progressing healthily forward.

Film Photography

I am a big fan of shooting landscapes and trees. Feel free to check out some of my work on my Instagram page. I started after inheriting my grandfather’s old Nikon FE2 and love all of the small movements inherent to the practice. I picked up this practice after I became frustrated with continually buying new cameras to meet clarity trends. I loved the imperfections in older photos and especially the ability to produce larger prints at a high quality.

Art

I have a love for pen and ink works of flora and fauna. There is nothing more exciting than seeing a pretty tree and drawing it. For the past year or so, I have been focusing on watercolors of landscapes. Most of this work is done with walnut ink and a sepia pen (I was inspired by a reproduction I saw once). Nature seems to be a nice balance to my everyday work :).

I hope to share some of them here sometime so check back periodically!
